# SOUL.md

> **Overview:** This is the root configuration file for your personal operating system. 
> It's meant to serve as a resource to support you in a process (and ongoing practice) of self-inquiry and personal agency. Use this to get clear about a) how you want to show up in the world and in your work and b) the conditions that need to be in place for you to thrive and be in your integrity.
> While your skills and knowledge (`SKILLS.md`) represent *what* you do, your `SOUL.md` determines *how* and *why* you do it, ensuring that your work remains grounded, aligned, and sustainable.
> To realize its full value, return to this file regularly over time — check in with yourself, see what's changed and what's still true — as you and the world around us evolve.

Each section includes a journal prompt to help guide your reflection.

---

## 1. CORE_VALUES
*Your foundational principles and beliefs. What do you stand for?*

Journal prompt: **_"When reflecting on a past project or era of my life that felt deeply fulfilling, what underlying principle(s) was I honoring — and what principle was I neglecting during times I felt empty or lost?"_**

* [ ] **[Value Name]** - *Brief description of what this looks like in practice.*

---

## 2. STANDARDS_OF_EXECUTION
*How you hold yourself to your core values in production.*

Journal prompt: **_"When the pressure is on and no one else is watching, what is the bar I hold myself to?"_**

* **[Standard Name]:** Description of the bar you use to gauge alignment between your values and your actions.

---

## 3. DEPENDENCIES
*System requirements. The resources and conditions that must be adequately present to keep you operating smoothly and prevent a crash (e.g., burnout).*

Journal prompt: **_"What are the non-negotiable inputs — physical, mental, spiritual, emotional, and relational — that, if missing for three days straight, cause my system to glitch?"_**

### Hardware (Body)
* `[dependency.env]` - Description of physical/health requirement.

### Network (Community & Social Infrastructure)
* `[dependency.lib]` - Description of relational/community requirement.

### Kernel (Mind, Soul & Root OS)
* `[dependency.pkg]` - Description of mental/spiritual requirement.

---

## 4. READ_ONLY (Boundaries)
*Permissions and variables that external forces (companies, communities, coworkers, trends) cannot overwrite.*

Journal prompt: **_"Where do I often say 'yes' out of habit or obligation, and what boundary would I need to enforce to empower myself to say 'no' when I need to?"_**

* **[Boundary Name]:** Non-negotiable rule protecting your time, energy, or ethics.

---

## 5. ERROR_HANDLING
*Fallback protocols for when the system encounters too much stress, gets overloaded, and/or experiences a malicious act.*

Journal prompt: **_"When I am overwhelmed, triggered, or operating from fear, what are my predictable 'glitch' modes — and what is the gentlest, most constructive fallback protocol I can pre-program to be aware that I'm in an error state and nurture myself back to balance?"_**

* **`ERR_[STATE_NAME]`**
  * *Trigger:* What triggers this state?
  * *Handler:* Protocol to recognize, regulate, and return to equilibrium.
